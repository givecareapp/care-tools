# GC-SDOH: GiveCare Social Determinants of Health Assessment

## Overview

The GC-SDOH is a comprehensive 28-question assessment tool designed to identify social determinants of health impacting caregivers and care recipients. This evidence-based screening tool helps connect individuals with appropriate resources and support services.

## Assessment Time
Approximately 10-15 minutes

## Domains Assessed
- Food Security
- Housing Stability
- Transportation Access
- Social Support Networks
- Financial Resources
- Education & Digital Access
- Safety & Legal Concerns
- Cultural & Language Needs
- Caregiver-Specific Factors

---

## Assessment Questions

### Food Security

**Question 1:** How often in the past 12 months were you worried whether your food would run out before you got money to buy more?

**Response Options:**
- Never
- Rarely
- Sometimes
- Often
- Always

**Scoring:** Often/Always indicates food insecurity risk

---

**Question 2:** How often in the past 12 months did the food you bought not last and you didn't have money to get more?

**Response Options:**
- Never
- Rarely
- Sometimes
- Often
- Always

**Scoring:** Often/Always indicates food insecurity

**Domain Resources:** Food banks, SNAP benefits, meal delivery programs, nutrition assistance

---

## Scoring & Interpretation

### Risk Levels

**Low Risk:** Few or no concerning responses across domains
- Action: Provide general resources and preventive information

**Moderate Risk:** Some concerning responses in 1-3 domains
- Action: Targeted resource referrals and follow-up assessment

**High Risk:** Multiple concerning responses across 3+ domains or critical needs (safety, housing instability)
- Action: Immediate resource connection, case management referral, safety planning

### Priority Areas

Certain responses trigger immediate action:
- Intimate partner violence (Q21: Yes)
- Housing instability/homelessness (Q3/Q4)
- Unable to afford medication (Q13: Yes)
- Severe food insecurity (Q1-2: Always)
- No emergency transportation (Q7: No)
- Extreme financial hardship (Q12: Extremely hard)

## Implementation Guidelines

### Administration
- Can be self-administered or provider-assisted
- Available in multiple languages
- Accessible format options (large print, audio, digital)
- Private, confidential setting

### Follow-Up
- Resource referrals within 48 hours
- Follow-up assessment at 3, 6, and 12 months
- Care plan integration
- Track resource utilization and outcomes

### Privacy & Consent
- Obtain informed consent before administration
- Explain purpose and how information will be used
- Allow question refusal without penalty
- Secure data storage and transmission
- HIPAA compliance

## Evidence Base

The GC-SDOH draws from:
- PRAPARE (Protocol for Responding to and Assessing Patients' Assets, Risks, and Experiences)
- AHC (Accountable Health Communities) screening tool
- U.S. Household Food Security Survey Module
- NAM (National Academy of Medicine) SDOH framework
- CDC Social Determinants of Health guidelines
- Caregiver-specific research and assessments
